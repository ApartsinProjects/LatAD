LDT — Latent Dynamics Twin

A frozen, self-supervised encoder of trajectory dynamics that augments the window-based MIIM detector (CVaDE). Companion to the main framework (C1–C4).

1. Why: the snapshot limitation

The window/mode detector (VaDE + C1–C4) sees a snapshot: it has no memory of the trajectory that led to the current window. Two anomaly classes are invisible to any snapshot (even a longer window or a convolution over it): (i) a state that is normal now but reached via an invalid path (bad transition), and (ii) a slow drift whose evidence spans far more than one window. The fix is a learned sufficient statistic of history at bounded dimension — a recurrent/ state-space context $c_t$ made meaningful by a prediction objective.

They train separately (stable, testable in isolation) and compose by a single concatenation. First verify "$c_t$ improves detection" in isolation, then add transitions and C1–C4.

3. LDT architecture (causal)

  • per-step encoder $e:\ \phi(W_t)\mapsto u_t$;
  • state model $f$ with a multi-timescale backbone (dilated temporal-conv / S4-Mamba / hierarchical "clockwork" recurrence): $\;c_t=f(c_{t-1},u_t,\tilde\tau_t)$;
  • saturating time-from-start $\tilde\tau_t=\log\!\big(1+(t-t_{\text{start}})\big)$ so early predictions widen (the state is under-determined near a trajectory start);
  • multi-horizon projection heads $g_h$ used only during training.

4. Training objective

Self-supervised contrastive multi-horizon prediction (InfoNCE), on normal data only, over horizons $h\in\{1,4,16,64,256,\dots\}$ (multiscale = assumption A8):

$$\mathcal L_{\text{LDT}}=\sum_h \lambda_h\ \text{InfoNCE}\big(g_h(c_t,\tilde\tau_t),\,u_{t+h},\,\{\text{negatives}\}\big)\ +\ \beta_{\text{vic}}\,\text{VICReg}(c_t).$$
  • The head scores compatibility of $(c_t,\text{future})$ pairs — it models the distribution of valid futures, so a valid-but-unpredictable transition (in-support) is not penalised; only out-of-support pairs are.
  • Contrastive form ⇒ collapse is prevented by the loss (a constant $c_t$ cannot win the discrimination; InfoNCE lower-bounds $I(c_t;\text{future})$, which is $0$ under collapse). VICReg guards against dimensional collapse; a light density/reconstruction anchor covers non-predictive observation anomalies.

4.1 Long-memory requirements (design against short-memory bias)

A naive next-step model forgets: the persistence signal dominates and $c_t$ keeps only the last few steps. To force long-term transitions/trends into $c_t$:

  • Long + curriculum horizons — include genuinely long $h$, weighted enough that the model cannot ignore them (far prediction is impossible from short memory); grow $h$ over training.
  • Multi-timescale backbone — dilated-TCN / SSM / clockwork, not a vanilla RNN.
  • Slow/trend targets — predict the future moving-average or slope at long horizons, not just raw values (Slow-Feature principle); optionally feed long-EMA / long-window slope as input.

What "long-term" should mean: a fixed-dimensional $c_t$ must summarise — the recent sequence of regimes, their dwell times, and the slow trend — not raw history. That summary is exactly what makes long-range invalid patterns (an invalid regime path, a drifting-out-of-spec trend) detectable.

4.2 Data & optimisation protocol

  • Normal only; reset $c$ and $\tau$ at run/file boundaries; burn-in context before scoring.
  • Teacher-force the state; open-loop the heads; per-channel standardisation on train.
  • Adam ~1e-3, gradient clipping, subsequence batches (len ~100–500+ for long memory).
  • Label-free model selection: state dim, context length, horizons chosen by held-out normal contrastive loss (+ detection on C1-generated anomalies).

4.3 Acceptance test — measure the memory horizon

Do not assume long memory; probe it. Train linear probes to recover long-past events / long-horizon futures from $c_t$ and measure the effective memory horizon. If probes cannot recover events from far back, increase horizons / change backbone. This is the LDT's gate before it feeds CVaDE.

5. Outputs (frozen) and how CVaDE uses them

  • Context $c_t$ — concatenated to the window: CVaDE runs on $\xi_t=[\phi(W_t)\oplus c_t\,(\oplus\,\Delta c_t)]$, standardised so neither part swamps. The discovered modes become (operating regime × history phase).
  • Transition score $s^{\text{trans}}_t=-\log\ \text{compat}(c_{t-1},u_t)$ — the transition improbability, added as its own anomaly term (not recoverable from the static $c_t$).

Final detector score: whitened reconstruction + nearest-mode latent NLL + basin instability (CVaDE) $+\ s^{\text{trans}}_t$, each standardised on normal and summed.

6. Training pipeline

  1. Train LDT on normal (contrastive multi-horizon) → pass the memory-horizon probe → freeze.
  2. Compute $c_t$ for all data; form $\xi_t=[\phi(W_t)\oplus c_t]$.
  3. Train CVaDE on normal $\xi_t$ (VaDE + anti-collapse); fit whitener, C3 ensemble, C4 thresholds.
  4. Detect: CVaDE score $+\ s^{\text{trans}}_t$.

7. Grounding

Composes established ideas rather than reinventing: Contrastive Predictive Coding (van den Oord 2018); recurrent state-space / world models (RSSM, PlaNet/Dreamer); long-range sequence models (S4, Mamba); forecasting-based CPS AD (OmniAnomaly, GDN, TranAD); classical state-space/Kalman; digital twins. Novel angle: the MIIM mode/regime structure and the four components layered on the learned latent dynamics.

Open questions / to discuss

  • Backbone choice: dilated-TCN vs SSM (Mamba/S4) vs hierarchical RNN — memory vs simplicity vs deps.
  • Negatives for InfoNCE: in-batch across time vs across runs vs hard negatives (near-in-time).
  • Does $c_t$ risk dropping non-predictive anomaly signal? How strong an anchor is needed.
  • Two-stage frozen vs end-to-end fine-tuning of LDT+CVaDE.
  • Where transitions live: $s^{\text{trans}}_t$ scalar vs stacking last-$k$ latents into CVaDE vs both.
  • Non-stationarity across runs / sensor drift — per-run normalisation vs learned regime of "session".
